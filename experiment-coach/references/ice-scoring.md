# ICE + Pain Scoring Rubric

When you have multiple experiments competing for attention, ICE + Pain gives you a way to compare them. Score each dimension 1-10. The individual scores matter more than the total — a high-impact, low-confidence experiment needs a different approach than a low-impact, high-confidence one.

## Impact
*If this works or if this is true, how impactful will it be on our goals?*

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Negligible | Almost no effect on acquisition |
| 2 | Minimal | Barely noticeable customer impact |
| 3 | Low | Small improvement, limited influence |
| 4 | Mild | Helpful but not meaningful |
| 5 | Moderate | Noticeable effect, not decisive |
| 6 | Meaningful | Clear benefit, still partial |
| 7 | High | Strong boost toward customers |
| 8 | Very High | Major movement toward closing deals |
| 9 | Critical | Unlocks key barrier to sales |
| 10 | Transformational | Determines ability to win customers |

## Confidence
*How sure we are that this assumption is true based on evidence and will replicate?*

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Guess | Just an idea, no data |
| 2 | Hunch | One weak signal |
| 3 | Pattern | Heard it a few times |
| 4 | Evidence | Clear repeated signal in interviews |
| 5 | Confirmed | Strong recurring pattern |
| 6 | Measured | Some data backs it up |
| 7 | Tested | Early tests show pull |
| 8 | Action | Proof that customers engage and act |
| 9 | Paying | Strong buying signals |
| 10 | Repeatable | Proven and repeatable |

## Ease
*How little effort, time or coordination is required to test or deliver this?*

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Extremely Hard | Requires major time, skills, access |
| 2 | Very Hard | Heavy lift with significant blocker |
| 3 | Hard | Multi-step, slow, resource intensive |
| 4 | Difficult | Noticeable friction and dependencies |
| 5 | Moderate | Achievable but needs coordination |
| 6 | Manageable | Straightforward with some minor hurdles |
| 7 | Easy | Quick, simple, few dependencies |
| 8 | Very Easy | Fast, low effort, minimal risks |
| 9 | Trivial | Almost no effort required |
| 10 | Instant | Can do immediately with tools |

## Severity of Need / Pain
*How intensely our customer feels this problem and how urgently do they want it solved?*

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | No pain | They don't care at all |
| 2 | Mild | Annoying but easy to ignore |
| 3 | Frustration | Noticed, but workaround exists |
| 4 | Inconvenience | Slows them down sometimes |
| 5 | Problem | They'd like it fixed soon |
| 6 | Costly | Wastes real time or money |
| 7 | Painful | Hurts performance or outcomes |
| 8 | Critical | Top 3 problem they'd pay to fix |
| 9 | Urgent | Must fix now or serious consequences |
| 10 | Existential | Business or safety at risk |

## How to use it

Pay attention to the Pain score specifically — it's the dimension most founders under-weight. An experiment targeting a problem customers score 8+ on pain is almost always worth running, even if ease is low.

A Confidence score above 5 should have real evidence behind it — not gut feeling, not "we heard it once", not "it makes sense logically." If you can't point to specific data, the score is probably too high. This is the dimension teams are most dishonest about.
